Who We Are

Breaking the Cycle of Poverty, One Child at a Time

Foundation for African Children Education (FACE) was started in 2006 in Vancouver, BC, Canada. A subsidiary of Focus on Africa Society — a registered Canadian charity (CRA) — FACE provides children in rural Ghana the chance to attend primary school. We believe education is the most powerful force to break poverty's cycle.

Primary Education in Rural Ghana

We focus on rural areas where the need is greatest and where development trickles down last. Children aged 5–12 who could not otherwise attend school.

Direct Donor-to-Child Connection

Donors have direct contact with their sponsored child. They receive a photo and see firsthand the intellectual and social progress their generosity creates.

Maximum Impact, Minimal Overhead

By leveraging willing volunteers and cutting back on third parties, we ensure over 80% of every donated dollar reaches the children directly.

The Process

How Your Donation Works

From your donation to a child's first day of school — here is exactly what happens, step by step.

1

You Give

Make a one-time or monthly donation through CanadaHelps — Canada's trusted secure giving platform. Tax receipt issued automatically.

2

A Child Is Chosen

Our local selection committee in each Ghanaian village — led by a retired teacher or pastor and a community elder — identifies children aged 5–12 most in need.

3

We Deliver Directly

Volunteers travel to Ghana and hand-deliver school kits — uniforms, sandals, backpack, textbooks, exercise books — plus lunch money to the parent. One-on-one. Face to face.

4

You See the Impact

We bring you a photo of your sponsored child. You see their face. You know their name. You watch them grow — and you know your donation made the difference.

Leadership

The People Behind FACE

A dedicated board of accomplished individuals united by one belief — that every child in Africa deserves the chance to go to school.

Founder & Director
Our Founder

George Piprah

George grew up in Ghana and immigrated to Canada in 1977, where he completed his MBA at the University of British Columbia and became a Certified General Accountant. He established a successful company and served as the former Honorary Consul General of Ghana in British Columbia for 12 years. Driven by his deep connection to his homeland and a conviction that education is the key to breaking the cycle of poverty, George founded F.A.C.E. in 2006. He is also a board member of Eyes for the World (EFTW), a Canadian Charity dedicated to preventing blindness worldwide.

Committee Member

Don Wolfe

A successful businessman in Vancouver, BC, originally from Jamaica, Don has been passionate about the plight of the underprivileged throughout his life. He supports a group feeding the homeless on Vancouver's streets and is also a member of Eyes for the World (EFTW).

Committee Member

Joseph Frimpong

Joseph founded Focus on Africa Society more than two decades ago out of a desire to support development in Africa. A BCIT graduate, he works as a Technical Manager at Shaw Cable Systems and currently serves as the Honorary Consul General of Ghana in BC.

Committee Member

Rohit Lal

Rohit is a successful businessman and owner of West Coast Beauty Co Ltd. He and his wife have been strong and unwavering supporters of F.A.C.E. since its very inception in 2006, playing a significant role in fundraising efforts over the years.

✦ In Loving Memory
Former Committee Member

Suzanne Eyers

1941 – 2019. Suzanne visited Ajura, Ghana when she was in her early sixties and fell deeply in love with the place and its people. Her profound passion for the welfare of children was evident every time she spoke of Ghana. She is deeply missed.
